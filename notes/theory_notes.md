# Theory Notes

This project develops a grey-zone sovereignty framework for understanding Taiwan-related financial-market reactions. The core claim is that geopolitical risk does not automatically translate into persistent market damage. Instead, financial reactions depend on how investors interpret a given shock.

Taiwan's unresolved sovereignty status produces recurring military, diplomatic, and geoeconomic pressure. These events are not isolated crises; they are part of a repeated grey-zone environment in which markets learn, adjust, and distinguish between different kinds of risk.

Key interpretive mechanisms:

- **Threat:** investors price events as direct military, diplomatic, or sanctions-related danger.
- **Opportunity:** investors interpret Taiwan's role as strategically valuable, especially in semiconductors.
- **Adaptation:** repeated events may generate weaker reactions as markets become accustomed to recurring pressure.
- **Surprise:** unanticipated events may produce stronger abnormal returns than expected or repeated events.
