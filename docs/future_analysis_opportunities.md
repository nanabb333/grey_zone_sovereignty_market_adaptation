# Future Analysis Opportunities

> Academic Research Notice:
> This document identifies possible analyses after dataset expansion is complete. It does not perform those analyses and does not report CAR results.

## Interpretation Type Versus CAR

Once the expanded dataset is validated, the project could compare market reactions across `Threat`, `Opportunity`, `Mixed`, and `Adaptation` events. This would help assess whether markets respond differently to coercive risk, supportive industrial policy, and repeated geopolitical signaling.

## State Support Versus CAR

The V3 expansion should add more state-support observations, including CHIPS Act, European Chips Act, TSMC Japan, and China Big Fund III events. Future analysis could examine whether high state-support signals are associated with more positive abnormal returns for strategically important semiconductor assets.

## Strategic Importance Versus CAR

Future analysis could test whether events coded as `High` strategic importance produce stronger or more persistent reactions than medium- or low-importance events. This is especially relevant for export controls, foundry chokepoints, and critical-materials controls.

## Event-Family Comparisons

The expanded dataset would support comparisons across:

- military exercises
- export controls
- industrial policy
- strategic investment
- economic coercion
- critical-materials controls
- diplomatic recognition

This could clarify whether markets react most strongly to physical security risk, technology restrictions, public subsidies, or diplomatic pressure.

## Repeated-Event Analysis

The existing dataset already contains repeated military-signaling events. V3 would add repeated export-control and policy-escalation events. Future analysis could evaluate whether market reactions decline as event families become more familiar, or whether escalation renews investor attention.

## Surprise Analysis

Expanded surprise coding could distinguish scheduled events from unexpected policy shocks. Future analysis could compare high-, medium-, and low-surprise events while controlling for event family. This would directly support the dissertation's adaptation-versus-anticipation discussion.

## Diplomatic Recognition Competition

Recognition switches by Honduras and Nauru, plus the U.S. TAIPEI Act, create a small but meaningful diplomatic-competition family. Future analysis could assess whether diplomatic isolation events have weaker immediate market effects but stronger narrative or risk-premium relevance.

## Export-Control Escalation Patterns

The expanded export-control sequence could support escalation analysis across:

- unilateral U.S. controls
- allied tool controls
- China countermeasures
- Taiwan export-control alignment
- critical-materials restrictions

Future work could examine whether investors interpret each new step as a fresh shock, as confirmation of a known decoupling path, or as evidence of policy entrenchment.

## Research Boundary

All future analyses should be performed only after event validation, source documentation, trading-date mapping, and theory-coding review are complete. Candidate events should not be included in statistical summaries until promoted into the validated dataset.
