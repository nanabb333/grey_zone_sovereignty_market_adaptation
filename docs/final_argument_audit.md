# Final Argument Audit

> Academic Research Notice:
> This document evaluates the dissertation argument for final portfolio readiness.
> It does not add new evidence or causal claims.

## Research Question

The research question is strong:

```text
Why do some Taiwan-related geopolitical shocks produce negative financial-market reactions while others generate muted or positive abnormal returns?
```

It is analytically clear, empirically answerable through event-study evidence, and relevant to international relations, international political economy, financial markets, and policy research.

## Puzzle

The puzzle is credible. Taiwan is a high-risk geopolitical flashpoint, so one might expect Taiwan-related shocks to damage markets. The empirical pattern is more conditional: risk events are negative on average, strategic-importance events are positive on average, and individual cases vary substantially.

## Theory

The theory is the strongest part of the dissertation. Grey-zone sovereignty provides a structural explanation for why Taiwan-related shocks recur. Investor interpretation explains why market reactions are not automatic. Strategic importance explains why some Taiwan-related shocks may be read as opportunity rather than only threat.

## Mechanism

The central mechanism is investor adaptation. This is plausible but also the most vulnerable part of the argument. Adaptation is inferred from market patterns rather than directly observed. The project has improved by explicitly separating adaptation from anticipation and by adding surprise and repetition coding.

## Measurement

The measurement strategy is transparent. The project uses event classifications, abnormal returns, CAR windows, and qualitative coding variables. The addition of `surprise_score`, `event_family`, and `event_repetition_level` strengthens measurement credibility.

Measurement remains limited because surprise and repetition are qualitative coding decisions, not direct measures of investor beliefs.

## Evidence

The evidence is sufficient for a master's-level exploratory event study. It supports the broad claim that geopolitical risk does not automatically produce uniform market damage.

The evidence is not sufficient for a strong causal claim that adaptation caused specific market reactions.

## Limitations

The limitations are clear and should remain visible:

- small event sample
- event-window overlap
- benchmark sensitivity
- possible prior pricing
- no direct investor-belief data
- manual event coding

These limitations do not invalidate the project. They define the appropriate scope of inference.

## Contribution

The contribution is strong when framed carefully:

```text
The dissertation develops a grey-zone sovereignty framework showing that Taiwan-related geopolitical risk is mediated by investor interpretation rather than mechanically translated into market damage.
```

## Strongest Claim

The strongest claim is:

```text
Taiwan-related geopolitical shocks have conditional market effects shaped by investor interpretation, strategic importance, surprise, and repetition.
```

This claim is well supported by the research design and results.

## Weakest Claim

The weakest claim is:

```text
Investors adapted to Taiwan-related geopolitical shocks.
```

This should be phrased as:

```text
The findings are consistent with investor adaptation, but they do not prove adaptation.
```

## Overclaimed Sections

Potentially overclaimed sections are any passages that imply adaptation has been directly identified or causally proven. These should be revised to include anticipation, prior pricing, and information leakage as rival explanations.

## Underdeveloped Sections

The most underdeveloped section is the empirical integration of surprise. The framework and dataset now exist, but the final manuscript should explain how surprise changes interpretation of the results.

## Final Assessment

The dissertation is credible as a theory-building event study. Its final strength depends on disciplined language: strong on interpretation and descriptive evidence, cautious on causal identification.
