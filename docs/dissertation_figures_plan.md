# Dissertation Figures Plan

> Academic Research Notice:
> This document is provided for educational and research reference purposes only.
> It proposes figures for dissertation revision and presentation.

## Purpose

This memo proposes four dissertation figures that would strengthen the clarity of the theoretical argument, empirical comparison, and adaptation-versus-anticipation discussion. The figures are designed to support the dissertation's existing framework rather than redesign the project.

## Figure 1: Grey-Zone Sovereignty Framework

### Purpose

Show the dissertation's core theoretical sequence:

```text
Grey-Zone Sovereignty
-> Recurring Taiwan-Related Shocks
-> Investor Interpretation
-> Market Reaction
```

The figure should emphasize that investor interpretation is the mediating step between objective geopolitical events and financial outcomes.

### Variables / Concepts

- Grey-zone sovereignty
- Recurring Taiwan-related shocks
- Investor interpretation
- Surprise
- Adaptation
- Market reaction
- Risk and strategic importance mechanisms

### Visualization Type

Conceptual flow diagram with mediating variables. Surprise and adaptation should appear as filters that shape investor interpretation.

### Dissertation Placement

Chapter 3: Theory Framework.

## Figure 2: Risk vs Strategic Importance CAR Comparison

### Purpose

Summarize the main empirical contrast between `Risk` events and `Strategic_Importance` events. This figure should communicate the dissertation's central finding that geopolitical risk does not automatically generate persistent market damage.

### Variables

- Event classification: `Risk`, `Strategic_Importance`
- TWSE CAR
- TSMC CAR
- Event window, preferably 3-day or 7-day CAR

### Visualization Type

Grouped bar chart comparing average CAR by event classification and market outcome.

### Dissertation Placement

Chapter 5: Results.

## Figure 3: Event Repetition vs CAR

### Purpose

Visualize whether repeated event families are associated with weaker, more mixed, or less negative market reactions. This figure would support the adaptation discussion without claiming definitive causal proof.

### Variables

- Event repetition level
- Event family
- TWSE CAR
- TSMC CAR
- Event label or year

### Visualization Type

Scatter plot or small-multiple plot. The x-axis can represent `event_repetition_level`, while the y-axis represents CAR. Points can be colored by event family or mechanism.

### Dissertation Placement

Chapter 5: Results or Chapter 6: Discussion.

## Figure 4: Adaptation vs Anticipation Logic

### Purpose

Clarify the strongest rival explanation. The figure should show why adaptation and anticipation can both produce muted CARs, while relying on different mechanisms.

### Variables / Concepts

- Pre-event expectations
- Day-0 event
- Prior pricing
- Investor learning
- Event repetition
- Surprise
- Observed CAR

### Visualization Type

Two-path logic diagram:

```text
Path A: Repeated shocks -> investor learning -> changed interpretation -> muted CAR
Path B: Prior warnings -> pre-event pricing -> limited Day-0 reaction -> muted CAR
```

The figure should include a note that the current evidence is consistent with adaptation but does not fully rule out anticipation.

### Dissertation Placement

Chapter 6: Discussion or Limitations.

## Implementation Note

Figures 1 and 4 are conceptual and can be created without new calculations. Figures 2 and 3 should be based on existing event-study output and any future event-repetition coding. Figure 3 should not be presented as causal unless additional evidence supports identification.
