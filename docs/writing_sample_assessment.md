# Writing Sample Assessment

> Academic Research Notice:
> This document assesses the dissertation as a master's-level writing sample.
> It is intended for portfolio preparation and application strategy.

## Overall Assessment

As a writing sample, the dissertation is strong. It demonstrates research design, interdisciplinary thinking, theory-building, data interpretation, and awareness of limitations. It is especially suitable for master's programs in international relations, international political economy, political economy, public policy, security studies, political risk, and business analytics.

## Strengths

1. Clear research puzzle with real-world relevance.
2. Strong Taiwan domain knowledge.
3. Original theoretical framing through grey-zone sovereignty.
4. Good connection between political events and financial-market evidence.
5. Transparent methodology and reproducible supporting repository.
6. Mature handling of limitations after adding adaptation-versus-anticipation discussion.
7. Strong fit for policy research and analytics-oriented graduate programs.

## Weaknesses

1. Adaptation remains inferred rather than directly observed.
2. Event sample is small.
3. Surprise coding is qualitative and should be carefully defended.
4. Some sections may still read as broader than the evidence allows.
5. The empirical design is descriptive rather than causally identified.

## Likely Reviewer Concerns

### Concern 1: Does the event study prove adaptation?

Answer:
No, and it should not claim to. The better claim is that the evidence is consistent with adaptation and supports a conditional interpretation of geopolitical risk.

### Concern 2: Is the sample too small?

Answer:
The sample is small, but appropriate for an exploratory dissertation if the project is framed as theory-building rather than definitive statistical testing.

### Concern 3: Are market movements really caused by Taiwan events?

Answer:
The project uses benchmark-adjusted returns to improve interpretation, but it should acknowledge benchmark sensitivity and broader market conditions.

### Concern 4: Is surprise coding subjective?

Answer:
Yes, but the coding is transparent, documented, and used to qualify claims rather than to overstate them.

## Top 5 Improvements

1. Insert the adaptation-versus-anticipation section into the discussion chapter.
2. Add a short methodology subsection explaining `surprise_score`, `event_family`, and `event_repetition_level`.
3. Include the repetition vs CAR figure with cautious interpretation.
4. Add an appendix table showing representative event coding decisions.
5. Tighten the conclusion so it emphasizes conditional interpretation rather than causal proof.

## Writing Sample Recommendation

Submit a polished excerpt rather than the entire dissertation if the application asks for a shorter sample. The best excerpt would combine:

1. Introduction
2. Theory framework
3. Research design
4. A short results/discussion section showing adaptation vs anticipation

This would demonstrate both conceptual sophistication and empirical discipline.
