# Theory Findings Memo

> Academic Research Notice:
> This memo integrates the validated V3 dataset into the project's theoretical framework. It does not add events, create CAR values, or make causal claims.

## What Appears Stronger After V3

The strategic-importance layer is stronger after V3. The dataset now includes a clearer sequence of export-control and semiconductor industrial-policy events, including Huawei, SMIC, CHIPS Act, AI-chip controls, and China Big Fund III. This makes the project less dependent on military-signaling events and better aligned with a broader geopolitical competition framework.

The opportunity mechanism is also stronger. V2 already included TSMC investment and CHIPS-related terms, but V3 adds earlier and broader industrial-policy context. This helps the framework distinguish risk events from state-support and capacity-building events.

The mixed interpretation category becomes more credible. Export controls are not purely negative or positive: they can create revenue and compliance risks while also increasing the strategic value of allied semiconductor capacity.

## What Appears Weaker

State-support coverage remains limited. Only 8 of 25 events have a coded state-support signal, and only 3 are coded High. This is acceptable for conservative coding, but it means state support is still a selective dimension rather than a full-sample variable.

Diplomatic competition remains underdeveloped in the validated V3 file. The current sprint did not integrate recognition-diplomacy candidates, so the dataset still lacks a validated recognition-competition family.

CAR coverage is weaker for newly integrated V3 events. The V3 dataset has 25 validated rows, but the current CAR summary file covers only the 18 legacy events.

## What Remains Inconclusive

It remains inconclusive whether strategic-importance coding is associated with stronger market reactions. The available CAR-observed subset is too small and incomplete for the newly validated events.

It also remains inconclusive whether high state support reliably corresponds to positive market interpretation. The high-support category has three validated events, but only one currently has an observed CAR row.

The adaptation argument remains mostly grounded in repeated military signaling. V3 has not yet added enough repeated export-control or industrial-policy events with CAR outputs to compare adaptation across policy domains.

## Future Evidence Needed

- Event-window analytics for the seven net-new V3 events.
- Recognition-diplomacy validation to broaden diplomatic competition coverage.
- Additional state-support cases with source packets.
- Official-source supplementation for AI-chip controls and China Big Fund III where noted in validation reports.
- Confounding review for the 2020-05-15 Huawei FDP rule and TSMC Arizona announcement.

## Bottom Line

V3 strengthens the project's theoretical foundation by making strategic importance, export controls, and industrial policy more central. The strongest improvement is conceptual and coverage-based. The empirical CAR layer has not yet caught up to the expanded validated dataset.
