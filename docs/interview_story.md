# Interview Story

> Academic Research Notice:
> This document prepares a concise interview narrative for the three-repository portfolio.

## Prompt

Tell me about a project you are proud of.

## Answer

One project I am especially proud of is my Taiwan geopolitical risk analytics portfolio. It started as a dissertation question and became a three-part research, analytics, and product package.

The research question was: why do some Taiwan-related geopolitical shocks fail to generate persistent market damage, even though Taiwan is a major geopolitical flashpoint? I developed a grey-zone sovereignty framework to explain this puzzle. The idea is that Taiwan's unresolved sovereignty status produces recurring shocks, but markets do not react mechanically to objective risk. Investors interpret events through threat, strategic importance, surprise, anticipation, and adaptation.

The first part of the project was the academic research repository. I built an event-study design using Taiwan-related events from 2022 to 2025. I analyzed TWSE and TSMC abnormal returns around risk events and strategic-importance events. I also created an adaptation-versus-anticipation framework because I wanted to avoid overstating the causal claim. The evidence is consistent with investor adaptation, but it does not prove adaptation, so I added surprise and repetition coding to make the interpretation more transparent.

The second part was the analytics engine. I converted the manual dissertation workflow into a Python event-study pipeline. The engine takes event metadata and market data, calculates abnormal returns and cumulative abnormal returns, generates summaries, and produces reusable outputs. This showed that I could move from research design to reproducible analytics.

The third part was the dashboard product. I turned the event-study outputs into a dashboard-ready decision-support layer for a geopolitical risk analyst. The dashboard includes KPI cards, executive summaries, rule-based insights, historical comparisons, and LLM-ready context. I deliberately avoided forecasting or trading recommendations because the goal was responsible analyst support, not unsupported prediction.

The reason I am proud of the project is that it connects three skills: research, analytics, and product thinking. It shows that I can start with a complex international relations problem, translate it into structured data and reproducible analysis, and then package the results for a practical user. For me, that bridge from research to analytics to decision support is exactly the kind of work I want to keep doing.

## Short Version

I built a three-part Taiwan geopolitical risk portfolio that began as a dissertation and became an analytics product. Repo 1 develops the research argument and event study. Repo 2 turns the method into a Python analytics engine. Repo 3 turns the outputs into a dashboard-ready decision-support product. The project shows my ability to connect international relations research, financial analytics, and product-oriented communication.
