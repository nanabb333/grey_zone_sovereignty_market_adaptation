# Event Study Expansion Impact

> Academic Research Notice:
> This impact assessment discusses how fuller CAR coverage would affect interpretation. It does not calculate CAR values or make causal claims.

## Theory Analysis

Expanded CAR coverage would make the V3 theory analysis more representative. At present, theory-variable counts use all 25 validated V3 events, but CAR summaries cover only 18 events. The missing events are concentrated in export controls, industrial policy, and strategic investment, so current CAR summaries underrepresent the most important V3 additions.

## State Support Analysis

State support would benefit substantially from expanded CAR coverage. Two high-support events, CHIPS and Science Act Signed and China Big Fund III Established, do not currently have CAR outputs. Adding them would make the High support category less dependent on the single observed TSMC CHIPS Act preliminary-terms event.

## Strategic Importance Analysis

Strategic importance coverage would improve because all seven missing events are strategically relevant, and most are coded High. Adding CAR outputs for these events would make the strategic-importance analysis less sparse and more aligned with the expanded V3 dataset.

## Interpretation Analysis

Interpretation analysis would improve most for `Mixed` and `Opportunity` categories. Several missing export-control events are coded `Mixed`, while CHIPS Act, TSMC Arizona 2020, and China Big Fund III are coded `Opportunity`. Current CAR summaries therefore underrepresent the categories that V3 was designed to strengthen.

## Event-Family Analysis

Event-family analysis would improve most for:

- `Export_Control`
- `Industrial_Policy`
- `Strategic_Investment`

Military exercise coverage is already empirically stronger because the existing CAR layer includes all current military-signaling events. The expansion would rebalance empirical coverage toward the policy and semiconductor competition families.

## Bottom Line

Extending CAR coverage to all validated V3 events would make the empirical layer match the research dataset. The strongest gain would be reducing the current mismatch between theory coverage and CAR coverage.
