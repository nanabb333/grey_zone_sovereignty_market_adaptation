# Key Findings

## Public Summary

The findings suggest that Taiwan-related geopolitical shocks do not produce uniform market reactions.

The pattern is conditional:

- some risk events are associated with negative abnormal returns
- some repeated events show muted or positive abnormal returns
- strategic-importance events may be interpreted differently from pure risk shocks
- market reactions may reflect both objective risk and investor interpretation

## Main Interpretation

The results are consistent with an adaptation interpretation, especially for repeated grey-zone events.

However, the evidence does not prove adaptation.

The safer claim is:

```text
Taiwan-related geopolitical shocks have conditional market effects shaped by investor interpretation, strategic importance, surprise, and repetition.
```

## Academic Caution

The project does not claim that repetition automatically causes weaker market reactions.

The observed pattern is suggestive evidence. Anticipation, prior pricing, sample limitations, benchmark sensitivity, and broader market conditions remain important alternative explanations.
