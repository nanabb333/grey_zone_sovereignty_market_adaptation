# Research Question

## Public Summary

This project asks:

```text
Why do some Taiwan-related geopolitical shocks produce negative financial-market reactions while others generate muted or positive abnormal returns?
```

Taiwan is a major geopolitical flashpoint and a central node in global semiconductor production. A simple risk-damage view would expect Taiwan-related shocks to consistently harm markets. The project instead examines whether market reactions are conditional on event type, surprise, repetition, investor interpretation, and Taiwan's strategic importance.

## Why This Matters

Taiwan-related events matter for:

- international relations
- political economy
- semiconductor supply chains
- geopolitical risk analytics
- financial market interpretation

The project treats market reactions as evidence about investor interpretation, not as direct proof of geopolitical outcomes.

## Scope of Inference

The evidence is best read as suggestive and exploratory.

The findings are consistent with the idea that investors may adapt to repeated Taiwan-related shocks, but they do not prove adaptation. Anticipation, prior pricing, broader market conditions, and benchmark sensitivity remain important rival explanations.
