# Discussion and Limitations

## Public Summary

The project's strongest contribution is not a causal proof of investor adaptation.

Its contribution is a disciplined framework for showing that Taiwan-related geopolitical risk has conditional market effects.

Market reactions may reflect:

- objective geopolitical risk
- investor interpretation
- strategic importance
- surprise
- event repetition
- anticipation and prior pricing
- broader market or semiconductor-sector conditions

## Main Limitation

The project observes market outcomes, not investor beliefs directly.

Because investor beliefs are not directly observed, adaptation should be treated as a plausible interpretation rather than a proven causal mechanism.

## Additional Limitations

- small event sample
- event-window overlap
- benchmark sensitivity
- manual event coding
- possible information leakage before event windows
- broader market conditions may affect results
- strategic semiconductor-sector dynamics may interact with geopolitical risk

## Responsible Interpretation

The most defensible conclusion is:

```text
The evidence is consistent with market adaptation to repeated Taiwan-related shocks, but anticipation and prior pricing remain important rival explanations.
```

This framing keeps the research credible for academic review and useful for risk analytics.
